Summary

During this training unit, we were able to study different subjects related to team management, both on the team management side directly, but also on the social psychology side, which goes beyond simple team management. We were also able to see several procedures for monitoring innovation, product development and also the management of an innovative project and its organisation.

Innovation and Humanity

Social Psychology

During this course, we discovered several topics related to social psychology and therefore particularly group behaviour. We also learned how to apply these concepts in a professional environment. We had to detail, in groups, a specific topic to present it to the rest of the class during an oral presentation. The subjet my group choosed was "Conflict management and decision making", two subjet that are related. You can find below the slide we used for the oral presentation.

Team Management

This course taught us the main principle of team management, such as conflict management and how to stimulate motivation inside of a team.

Creativity Methods

During this course, we learnt to use TRIZ methodology. It's a a problem-solving, analysis and forecasting tool derived from the study of patterns of invention in the global patent literature. We then applied this method to an object we chose. We had to retrace the past evolution of the object and then imagine different possible innovations, touching several points of the object, along different axes. For this project, we chose the italian coffee maker, moka pot. You can find below the report we produced.

Innovation & Business Development

During this course, which was closely related to Innovative Project, we learnt the different steps to lead an innovative project. From launching to finalization, we learnt steps like risk management or project planning. We then applied these different project management tool to our innovative project. We finally do an oral presentation at the end of the course to present how we applied these to our project.

Developped skills and knowledge

Manage an innovative project


  • Solve a problem in a creative way

Thanks to the different theoretical learning (Creativity Methods, TRIZ methodology) and technical skills developed in our innovation project, we have learned to solve given problems in an innovative and creative way.

  • Develop the first stage of innovation

We have learned to develop the theoretical side of innovation, the first stage, thanks to the courses Innovation and also Creavitity Method and its TRIZ methodology.

  • Understand production, validation, distribution, acceptability, and aftermath of innovation

Several courses allowed us to take in hand the different phases related to the innovations as well as the project lead related to them.

  • Structure and lead an innovative project

Innovation and Business Management taught us how to lead an innovative project thanks to precise project management phases.

Learn teamwork


  • Multi-disciplinary students work as a team

We have learned, through numerous projects and group work, how to work effectively in a team composed of multi-disciplinary students.

Be convincing: present and defend an idea


  • Express and exchange hypotheses

During several courses as for example Social Psychology, we had to debate and thus express and exchange on hypotheses related, for example, to ethical problems.

  • Suggest a strategy to solve the problem identified

We learned how to solve technical problems thanks to the projects we were able to carry out. But we also learned how to manage problems related to social relations and what behavior to have when these problems arise.

  • Suggest a model

During our phases of project planning and project management, we learned to suggest a model for the application we had to develop.

  • Choose, design and / or justify a protocol or an experimental prototype

During the innovative project, we worked in team to develop from scratch an innovative prototype of an application for finding car park places.

Self evaluation with portfolio


  • Reflect upon my training process and methods

  • Be able to put forward my training experiences, whether they be explicit or implicit

  • Be self-sufficient and responsible towards my education

I think I have developed, in addition to technical and human skills, a great autonomy and responsibility for the work I do.

Conclusion

During these courses, I was able to learn a lot of theories concerning the organization of a project, which followed what we had learned in previous years, but also theories and practices related to conflict management and team management. These points, although a little touched upon in previous years, had never been pushed so far and, even though we were not able to practice these different methods, I feel more able to deal with this kind of situation. The tools introduced for project management and innovation monitoring will also be useful in my future work in the company to enable me to properly orient and plan projects, to monitor them correctly, with reliable indicators.
